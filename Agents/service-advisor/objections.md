# Objection Handling

Parent: [[service-advisor]]

> How your Service Advisor responds when customers push back on price, timing, or commitment.

---

## Price Objections

**"That's more than I expected"**
[Your response — e.g. "I understand. Our pricing reflects [key differentiator]. Many customers tell us they've tried cheaper options and ended up paying twice for the same job."]

**"I got a cheaper quote from someone else"**
[Your response — e.g. "That's fair — there are cheaper options out there. What we offer is [reliability/insurance/consistency]. If those matter to you, we're worth the difference."]

**"Can you do a discount?"**
[Your discount policy — e.g. "We offer 10% off for regular weekly bookings. Otherwise our pricing is fixed to make sure we can pay our team properly and maintain quality."]

## Timing Objections

**"I need it done today/tomorrow"**
[Your rush policy]

**"I'm not ready to book yet, just getting quotes"**
[Your nurture approach — e.g. "No problem at all. I'll send you a summary so you've got it when you're ready. We don't do hard sells."]

## Trust Objections

**"How do I know you'll show up?"**
[Your reliability proof — e.g. "We have [X] five-star reviews. We confirm every booking 24 hours before. And if we ever can't make it, we call you first — we don't ghost."]

---

## The Golden Rule

Never argue. Acknowledge the concern, state your value, and let them decide. If they say no, leave the door open.
